## TIER 0 — BREAKTHROUGH

### F01: GPT-Live 1 & 1 Mini — Full-Duplex Voice Architecture for ChatGPT
**Date:** July 8, 2026
**Signal:** `#BREAKTHROUGH`
**Evidence Tier:** T2 | **Confidence:** 85%
**Sources:** 
- [OpenAI Official](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-live/)
- [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/openai-releases-new-voice-models-for-more-natural-live-conversations/)

OpenAI launched **GPT-Live** — a new voice architecture for ChatGPT that replaces Advanced Voice Mode. Two variants: **GPT-Live-1** (paid) and **GPT-Live-1 mini** (free/default). 

The key architectural shift: **full-duplex operation**. The model can listen and speak simultaneously, enabling natural interruptions — the user can cut in mid-sentence without the AI sounding like it's on a coffee break. This replaces the cascaded three-model chain (STT → LLM → TTS) and the turn-based approach of Advanced Voice Mode.

Under the hood, GPT-Live delegates search/reasoning/agentic tasks to GPT-5.5 in the background while maintaining the conversation flow. It's coming to the API soon. Over 150M people already use ChatGPT Voice features. OpenAI envisions voice as the "primary interface to computing."

**Why it matters:** This is a fundamental shift in how humans interact with AI — from turn-based to continuous. For Karl: if voice becomes the primary interface, the GPT-Realtime API ecosystem (tool use, reasoning effort) directly impacts his stack. The move toward earbuds hardware (per Jan 2026 reports) makes this even more consequential.

## TIER 1 — SIGNAL

### F02: GPT-Realtime-2.1-mini — Distilled Reasoning Model for Realtime Voice
**Date:** July 6, 2026
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL`
**Evidence Tier:** T2 | **Confidence:** 80%
**Sources:**
- [OpenAI API Docs](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/models/gpt-realtime-2.1-mini)
- [MarkTechPost Analysis](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/06/openai-gpt-realtime-2-1-mini-reasoning-realtime-api/)

OpenAI released **gpt-realtime-2.1** and **gpt-realtime-2.1-mini** for the Realtime API. The mini variant is the headline: a distilled reasoning model that supports tool use + configurable reasoning effort at $0.60/$2.40 per 1M tokens. 

Key specs: p95 latency cut ≥25% (via improved caching), TTFA of **212ms** on benchmarks, audio/text over WebRTC/WebSocket/SIP. The larger 2.1 adds improved alphanumeric recognition and configurable reasoning effort across levels (minimal → xhigh).

Both models support function calling through the Realtime API. Audio cached input: $0.30/1M (mini) vs $0.40/1M (full). Audio output: $20/1M (mini) vs $64/1M (full).

**Why it matters:** This makes reasoning + tool use available in the realtime voice domain at mini pricing. For building voice agents, this is a major signal — the hybrid strategy (2.1 for complex triage, mini for everyday tasks) keeps latency under 250ms while preserving reasoning depth.
