## 🔥 BREAKTHROUGH — Tier S

### DeepReinforce Releases Ornith-1.0: Self-Scaffolding Open-Weight Models for Agentic Coding (June 25–26)
- **What:** DeepReinforce released **Ornith-1.0**, an MIT-licensed open-weights family of models specifically designed for agentic coding tasks. Built on top of Gemma 4 + Qwen 3.5, it ships in three sizes: **9B Dense** (69.4% SWE-Bench Verified), **35B MoE** (75.6%), and **397B MoE** (82.4%). All at 262K context. The key innovation is a self-improving RL training framework that jointly optimizes both solution rollouts AND the task-specific scaffolds/harnesses that guide those rollouts — instead of relying on human-designed harnesses, the model learns to generate its own scaffolding.
- **Key benchmarks:** 397B at 82.4% SWE-Bench Verified and 62.2% SWE-Bench Pro beats Claude Opus 4.7 (80.8% / 64.3%) on Verified, trails on Pro. The 35B MoE at 75.6% SV significantly outperforms Qwen 3.5-397B (76.4% — close but Ornith is smaller). At Terminal-Bench 2.1, the 397B hits 77.5 vs Claude Opus 4.8's 85.0.
- **Karl-relevance:** HIGH. The 35B MoE and 9B Dense are directly runnable locally on M4 Max hardware. MIT license means full commercial/research freedom. The self-scaffolding RL approach is a genuine architectural novelty — not just another fine-tune. For agentic coding workflows (OpenClaw skills, local agent frameworks), this could be a significant local-first option. GGUF variants available on HF.
- **Signal:** #BREAKTHROUGH — First open-weight model family with self-improving RL scaffolding for agentic coding. Novel architecture, not incremental.
- **Sources:** [DeepReinforce Blog](https://deep-reinforce.com/ornith_1_0.html) | [HF Collection](https://huggingface.co/collections/deepreinforce-ai/ornith-10) | [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2026/06/openai-and-broadcom-announce-chip-designed-for-llm-inference-at-scale/) (secondary coverage)
- **Evidence:** T3 — verified via DeepReinforce blog post with full benchmark table, HuggingFace collection with 7 model variants
