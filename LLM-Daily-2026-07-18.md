### F01: Inkling — 975B MoE Multimodal from Thinking Machines
**Date:** July 14, 2026 (released)
**Signal:** `#BREAKTHROUGH` ⭐
**Evidence Tier:** T3 | **Confidence:** 85%
**Sources:**
- [HF: thinkingmachines/Inkling](https://huggingface.co/thinkingmachines/Inkling)
- [HF (unsloth GGUF): unsloth/inkling-GGUF](https://huggingface.co/unsloth/inkling-GGUF)

**Thinking Machines released Inkling**, a 975B total / 41B active sparse MoE multimodal model. It accepts text, image, and audio inputs and generates text outputs — a native multimodal architecture where all modalities are projected into a shared hidden space processed jointly by the decoder.

Key details:
- **66-layer decoder-only transformer** with sparse MoE (6 of 256 experts per token + 2 shared), hybrid local/global attention
- **975B total, 41B active parameters** — BF16 and NVFP4 numerics supported
- **Native multimodal**: images/video via hierarchical patch encoder; audio via discrete token encoding (16kHz WAV, under 20 min for optimal performance)
- **Open weights** with Apache 2.0 license
- Available via SGLang, vLLM, TokenSpeed, Unsloth, and HuggingFace Transformers
- Already #1 trending on HF (985 likes, 7,870 downloads as of this fetch)

**Benchmark scores** (effort=0.99, from r/acc leaderboard July 14):

**Independent check:** Read the HF model card (full page) and the unsloth GGUF quantization repo. The MoE architecture (6/256 + 2 shared) is consistent with modern sparse-expert models. Unsloth has published a GGUF quantization, making it locally runnable via llama.cpp.

**Why it matters:** Inkling is the most significant model launch in this sweep — a 975B MoE that's multimodal at the architecture level (not just an LLM with appended vision/audio adapters). ## TIER 1 — SIGNAL

### F02: ThinkingCap-Qwen3.6-27B — Token-Efficient Reasoning at 50% Less
**Date:** July 6, 2026 (released); trending now
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL` ⭐
**Evidence Tier:** T3 | **Confidence:** 80%
**Sources:**
- [HF: bottlecapai/ThinkingCap-Qwen3.6-27B](https://huggingface.co/bottlecapai/ThinkingCap-Qwen3.6-27B)

**A finetune of Qwen3.6-27B that reduces thinking tokens by an average of 45.8% while preserving (and slightly improving) accuracy.** Achieved via a minimally invasive finetuning on a curated problem set.

Key details:
- **Out-of-domain token efficiency**: GPQA-Diamond at 83.8% accuracy with only 3,351 thinking tokens (down from 10,777 — 67.8% reduction). SuperGPQA: 64.0% at 3,384 vs 8,246 (58.4% reduction).
- **In-domain**: GSM8K accuracy improved from 93.3% to 96.5%, thinking tokens down 74.1%.
- Truncation dropped from 2.9% to 0.4%; looping stays at ~0.2%.
- The blog post at bottlecapai.com/thinkingcap-qwen3-6-27b provides the full methodology.

**Independent check:** Read the HF model card in detail. The out-of-domain and in-domain tables are self-consistent — accuracy is preserved or improved while thinking tokens are dramatically reduced. This is a real token-efficiency win, not just a benchmark artifact: truncation dropping from 2.9% to 0.4% suggests the model actually terminates its reasoning traces more cleanly.

**Why it matters:** For Karl's local inference budget, this directly addresses the cost of thinking-mode models. A 46% average reduction in thinking tokens means significantly cheaper agentic loops. The fact that accuracy is preserved (and sometimes improved) makes this a genuine quality signal, not just a compression artifact.

### F03: Gepard 1.0 — Real-Time Streaming TTS with Voice Cloning
**Date:** June 22, 2026 (released); trending now
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL` ⭐
**Evidence Tier:** T3 | **Confidence:** 75%
**Sources:**
- [HF: nineninesix/gepard-1.0](https://huggingface.co/nineninesix/gepard-1.0)

**A ~556M-parameter autoregressive text-to-speech model built for real-time dialogue.** It's a single language model that learned text and speech together, generating audio piece by piece as text arrives — no waiting for full sentences.

Key details:
- **~25× real-time on a single RTX 5090**, with first audio chunk (TTFA) in ~50ms
- **One clean pass per frame** — the entire audio frame (32 orthogonal FSQ channels) sampled in one step, no depth-transformer
- **CFG refinement baked into weights** — normally a two-pass cost, now free
- **Voice cloning from short clips** — adds nothing to per-word cost
- **Backbone**: Qwen3.5 full-attention transformer (14 layers, hidden 1024, 8 heads) + NVIDIA NeMo NanoCodec (FSQ, 22.05kHz, 21.5 frames/s, 1.89 kbps)
- **Languages**: English (US/UK), Spanish (Mexico), Portuguese (Brazil), Dutch — plus 5 English accents
- **Benchmarks** (Seed-TTS-eval): WER 0.036, NISQA-MOS 4.25, DIS 4.51 — leads on perceived audio quality and naturalness
- **License**: Apache 2.0 (codec has NVIDIA Open Model License)
- One RTX Pro 6000 Blackwell (96GB) holds up to 256 conversations in parallel

**Independent check:** Read the HF model card fully. The benchmark table compares against VoxCPM2, Fish-S2, OmniVoice, Qwen3-TTS, Echo-TTS, and Chatterbox. Gepard leads on NISQA-MOS (4.25) and DIS (4.51), trading some speaker similarity for the cleanest naturalness — a design choice that makes sense for dialogue systems where voice matching matters less than natural flow.

**Why it matters:** For Karl's audio/voice pipeline needs, Gepard is a genuinely new option in the real-time TTS space. At ~556M params with 25× RT on a single GPU, it's feasible for local deployment. The streaming-first design (TTFA ~50ms) makes it competitive with commercial services like Cartesia.

## SYNTHESIS & CROSS-FINDING CONNECTIONS

**The Multimodal MoE Wave:** Inkling (975B MoE, multimodal at the architecture level) is the clearest signal of the day. It joins a growing set of models that treat modality as native rather than bolted-on: Qwen3-VL series, LLaVA-OneVision-2, and now Thinking Machines' approach where images/video and audio are encoded into a shared hidden space processed jointly by a single decoder. This is architecturally different from the "add a vision tower to an LLM" pattern — it's a genuinely multimodal architecture.

**Token Efficiency as a Frontier:** ThinkingCap's 46% average reduction in thinking tokens, combined with Inkling's open-weight + Apache 2.0 licensing and the unsloth GGUF quant, paints a picture of a maturing local inference ecosystem. The direction is clear: models are becoming both more capable (Inkling at 975B MoE) and cheaper to run locally (ThinkingCap's token efficiency, GGUF quants for everything).

**The Sub-1B Multi-Modal Trend Continues:** OvisOCR2 (0.8B), MOSS-Transcribe-Diarize (0.9B), Gepard (~556M), and GigaAM-Multilingual (220M) all share a pattern: doing complex multi-modal tasks with models under 1B parameters. This is the opposite direction from Inkling's 975B — showing that for specific domains, small specialized models still beat large general-purpose ones.

**Saturday Quiet Is Normal:** No major lab announcements on a Saturday. OpenAI posted two non-model articles (A Scorecard for the AI Age, Why Teens Deserve Safe AI). Anthropic's last post is June 30 (Claude Science + Fable 5 return). DeepMind's front page highlights SIMA 2, Genie 3, and robotics — no new model releases.
