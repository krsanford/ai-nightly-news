### 1b: Gemma 4 12B — Encoder-Free Unified Multimodal Model
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL` | **Date:** June 3, 2026 | **Sources:** lmstudio.ai, ollama.com, jonpeddie.com, wikipedia.org, medium.com/aimonks
**Confidence:** 85%

Google released **Gemma 4 12B** on June 3 — a unified, encoder-free architecture that handles text, image, and audio in a single model without separate encoders (eliminating the ~850M parameters typically used by dedicated vision/audio encoders). Released for pre-training and instruction-tuning. Available on Ollama and LMStudio.

**Key innovation:** Traditional multimodal AI uses separate encoder modules for images (550M params) and audio (300M params). Gemma 4 12B gets rid of these entirely, using a unified autoregressive decoder. The text-only variant with Per-Layer Embeddings can operate using less than 1 GB of memory.

**Karl-relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH. For local inference on M4 hardware, an encoder-free architecture that consolidates vision/audio/text into one model is architecturally interesting. The ~12B parameter count fits well on consumer hardware. If Karl uses multimodal tasks locally, this is worth testing.

**Evidence:** T3 (LMStudio, Ollama library listings, Jon Peddie Research analysis). T2 (Wikipedia entry, Medium deep-dive on encoder-free architecture).
