# LLM Research Digest — 2026-05-20

## BOTTOM LINE (READ THIS FIRST)

Yesterday was a defining day for the agentic AI race. **Google I/O dropped three major things at once: Gemini 3.5 Flash GA (83.6% MCP Atlas, fastest frontier model at 4x speed), Gemini Omni world model (first multi-modal input→video output system), and Antigravity 2.0 (agent-first dev platform with managed agents API).** Simultaneously, OpenAI filed confidential IPO papers targeting a September debut at $850B valuation — the most consequential corporate event in AI history. ## TREND WATCH

1. **Agent infrastructure is the primary battleground.** Google Antigravity 2.0, OpenAI Codex enterprise switching attack, and Anthropic's June 15 subscription split all converge on the same point: the agent layer (not just the model layer) is where competition happens now. OpenClaw is in the crosshairs of every major lab's pricing strategy. [Source: The Verge, CyberNews, APIDog]

2. **Speed + agentic benchmarks are the new benchmark standard.** Gemini 3.5 Flash's headline is "4x faster" — and MCP Atlas (83.6%) is now the dominant agentic benchmark. Terminal-bench, SWE-Bench Pro, OSWorld-Verified all measure agent behavior, not just chat quality. [Source: DeepMind models page]

3. **OpenAI IPO creates a new market dynamics layer.** $850B valuation sets the floor for what "frontier lab" means in financial terms. SpaceX's concurrent filing (post-$1.25T valuation) and Anthropic's $30B annualized revenue tripled since December create three-way competition for capital markets attention. [Source: CNBC, Bloomberg, Reuters]

4. **Anthropic is paying its way out of compute constraints.** The $1.25B/month SpaceX deal + 50% Claude Code limit increase + June 15 subscription split all point to the same story: Anthropic's compute bottleneck is real and expensive. Karpathy joining pre-training suggests they're betting on AI-assisted research efficiency over brute-force scaling. [Source: basenor.com, pasqualepillitteri.it]

5. **World models (Omni) are a different track from agentic LLMs.** Gemini Omni's approach — simulating physics/gravity/kinetic motion to generate video — is orthogonal to the agentic benchmark race. Whether it matters for Karl's work depends on whether multimodal world modeling becomes the next frontier after agentic text models. [Source: DeepMind, The Next Web]

## OPEN-SOURCE DEEP DIVE

### Open-Weight Landscape
- **Gemma 4** (April 2, 2026) — Purpose-built for advanced reasoning and agentic workflows. Google's open-weight answer to the frontier model race.
- **Kimi K2.6** — ~1.1T parameters, Modified MIT license. One of strongest open-weight coding models. [Source: HuggingFace blog]
- **Qwen 3.6-35B-A3B** — MoE architecture (35B total / ~3B active). Available via unsloth GGUF. Not yet working in Ollama due to separate mmproj vision files. [Source: Unsloth docs, HuggingFace]
- **GLM-4.7 Thinking** — Top open-weight coding model, free to self-host. DeepSeek's answer to GPT-5 coding competition.

### Inference Framework Support
- **vLLM v0.21.0** (May 15, 2026) — Apache 2.0. Primary production serving engine for 2026. Supports Gemini 3.5 series. [Source: Bizon Tech]
- **llama.cpp b9263** (May 21, 2026) — Active development; TurboQuant KV cache quantization discussion active on GitHub. [Source: GitHub releases]
- **Ollama** — Qwen 3.6 GGUF models exist but vision support incomplete (separate mmproj files). No working Qwen 3.6 GGUF in Ollama currently.

### Performance Tuning Signals
- **TurboQuant** (ggml-org/llama.cpp discussion #20969) — WHT + QJL + MSE combination for extreme KV cache quantization. March 2026 update shows active research.
- **Speculative decoding** — vLLM offers built-in support; production deployments increasingly use it for agentic workloads where latency matters.
- **Hardware reality check:** Qwen 3.6 Plus (hosted/larger tier) is NOT open-weight. Only the 35B-A3B MoE variant is available locally.
