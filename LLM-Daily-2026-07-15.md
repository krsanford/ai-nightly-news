## TIER 0 — BREAKTHROUGH

### F12: PrismML Bonsai 27B — First 27B-Class Model to Run on an iPhone
**Date:** July 14, 2026 (official release)
**Signal:** `#BREAKTHROUGH` ⭐
**Evidence Tier:** T3 | **Confidence:** 85%
**Sources:**
- [PrismML: PrismML Announces 1-bit Bonsai 27B](https://prismml.com/news/prismml-releases-bonsai-27b)
- [HuggingFace Collection: prism-ml/bonsai-27b](https://huggingface.co/collections/prism-ml/bonsai-27b)
- [GIGAZINE: Bonsai 27B Runs on iPhone](https://gigazine.net/gsc_news/en/20260715-bonsai-27b/)
- [Hacker News Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48910545)

**PrismML officially released Bonsai 27B**, a compressed version of Qwen3.6-27B (27 billion parameters) that runs on smartphones. Two variants:
- **1-bit Bonsai 27B**: 3.9 GB, effective precision of 1.125 bits per weight (binary weights {-1, +1}), ~89.5% performance retention vs. original Qwen3.6-27B
- **Ternary Bonsai 27B**: 5.9 GB, effective precision of 1.71 bits (ternary weights {-1, 0, +1}), ~94.6% performance retention

**Benchmark scores vs. original Qwen3.6-27B (85.0 overall):**
| Category | Original | Ternary | 1-bit |
|----------|----------|---------|-------|
| Math (GSM8K, MATH-500, AIME) | — | 93.4 | 91.7 |
| Coding (HumanEval+, MBPP+, LiveCodeBench) | — | 86.0 | 81.9 |
| Agent/tool invocation (BFCL v3, TauBench) | — | 74.0 | 66.0 |
| Knowledge/STEM (MMLU-Redux, MuSR) | — | 77.0 | 73.4 |
| Vision (MMMU Pro, OCRBench) | — | 65.2 | 59.6 |

**Performance on hardware:** 163 tok/s (RTX 5090, 1-bit), 87 tok/s (M5 Max, 1-bit), 11 tok/s (iPhone 17 Pro). Context window: ~262K tokens. Multimodal (accepts images).

**Available formats:** GGUF (llama.cpp), MLX (Apple Silicon), AWQ-4bit (image-text-to-text variants). Apache 2.0 license. Available on HuggingFace under the `prism-ml` collection.

**Why it matters:** This is a genuine compression breakthrough — not just "quantized model" but a practical demonstration that a 27B-class reasoning model can run on a phone. For Karl's M4 Max: 87 tok/s on the 1-bit build means interactive inference is feasible. The GGUF and MLX variants mean it works with llama.cpp and Ollama out of the box. This is the kind of model that makes "run something useful locally" genuinely practical for mobile use cases.

### F13: OpenAI GPT-Live Gets Deeper Coverage — Full-Duplex Voice Architecture
**Date:** July 8, 2026 (launched), coverage continues July 15
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL`
**Evidence Tier:** T2 | **Confidence:** 75%
**Sources:**
- [Panstag: GPT-Live Explained](https://www.panstag.com/2026/07/gpt-live-openai-voice-explained.html)
- [Mashable: OpenAI's GPT-Live Can Keep a Real Conversation](https://mashable.com/tech/openai-gpt-live)
- [Quasa: GPT-Live Voice Models Launch](https://quasa.io/media/openai-gpt-live-voice-models-launch-real-time-conversations-in-chatgpt)

GPT-Live launched July 8 as a full-duplex voice architecture — meaning ChatGPT can listen and speak simultaneously, not sequentially. Key architectural details:
- **Two models:** GPT-Live-1 (full model for Go/Plus/Pro), GPT-Live-1 mini (lighter for Free tier)
- **Continuous interaction layer** manages live back-and-forth; **delegation layer** routes harder questions to GPT-5.5 in the background while conversation continues
- **Audio-native safety evaluations** built specifically for voice (self-harm, psychosis, mania, emotional reliance, violence, sexual content) — not reused from text-based rules
- **No public API yet** — only available inside ChatGPT app (iOS, Android, web)
- Fully replaced the old Advanced Voice Mode (no fallback option)

**Why it matters:** Full-duplex voice is architecturally different, not just incrementally better. The background delegation to GPT-5.5 means the conversation layer stays fluid while deep reasoning happens off-path. For Karl: this reinforces the coding-agent convergence but also raises questions about API access — no public endpoint means you can't build on it yet. The audio-native safety evaluations are worth watching as a pattern for voice-specific guardrails.

## SYNTHESIS & CROSS-FINDING CONNECTIONS

**The Compression Arms Race Accelerates:** Bonsai 27B (F12) is the headline, but it's part of a larger pattern: models are getting both bigger AND smaller simultaneously. Qwen3.6-27B compressed to 3.9 GB while frontier models (GPT-5.6, Gemini 3.5 Pro, Grok 4.5) push context windows toward 2M tokens and MoE architectures. The tension is between "run anything anywhere" (Bonsai on iPhone) and "the biggest possible model" (Gemini 3.5 Pro at 2M context). For Karl's M4 Max: the Bonsai 1-bit build at 87 tok/s is genuinely useful for local inference, while the 2M-context frontier models remain API-only.

**Voice AI Is Going Full-Duplex:** GPT-Live (F13) represents a fundamental architectural shift — full-duplex voice means the model can truly listen and speak at once, not just stitch sequential STT→LLM→TTS. This is the same direction that Anthropic's Fable 5 extensions are heading (more capable, more interactive). The convergence: AI assistants are becoming genuinely conversational, not just command-response systems.

**Anthropic Is Building Ecosystem Lock-In:** Claude for Teachers (F14) is a strategic play — free access for educators creates pipeline dependency in K-12 classrooms. Combined with the Fable 5 extensions and the Cursor "honeycomb" leak (from July 14 coverage), Anthropic is positioning Claude as the default AI layer across multiple domains (coding via Cursor, education via Teachers, research via Fable). For Karl: this means Anthropic's API pricing and availability decisions will have outsized impact on the ecosystem.

**The July 17 Date Is a Stress Test:** Gemini 3.5 Pro's July 17 target (F15) is the most interesting uncertainty. If it launches on time, it validates Google's engineering under pressure. If it doesn't (and the "base model scrapped" claim holds), it confirms that deep architectural changes take longer than announced. Either way, Karl should prepare for both outcomes.
