## TIER 0 — BREAKTHROUGH

### F01: Ternary-Bonsai-27B — Full-Ternary Quantization at 1.71 Bits/Weight
**Date:** July 4, 2026 (released); trending July 17
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL` ⭐
**Evidence Tier:** T3 | **Confidence:** 85%
**Sources:**
- [HF: prism-ml/Ternary-Bonsai-27B-gguf](https://huggingface.co/prism-ml/Ternary-Bonsai-27B-gguf)
- [GitHub: PrismML-Eng/Bonsai-demo](https://github.com/PrismML-Eng/Bonsai-demo)

**Prism ML released Ternary-Bonsai-27B**, a 27B-class reasoning model quantized to true ternary weights ({−1, 0, +1}) at 1.71 bits per weight — no escape hatches to higher precision. Key details:
- **~7.2 GB deployed footprint** (down from ~54 GB FP16) — the full 27B-class reasoning on a laptop or single GPU
- **95% of FP16 intelligence retained**: 80.49 average across 15 thinking-mode benchmarks — outperforms IQ2_XXS build (72.73) at less than two-thirds the footprint
- Retains thinking, reasoning, and agentic behavior deep in the sub-4-bit regime: math within 2 points of full precision (93.40), coding at 85.96, agentic tool use at 74.01
- **End-to-end ternary**: embeddings, attention projections, MLP projections, and LM head all ternary; vision tower in compact 4-bit HQQ
- **262K-token context** on-device via Qwen3.6-27B hybrid-attention backbone (~75% linear attention) + 4-bit KV cache quantization
- Ships with a **DSpark speculative-decoding drafter** for a lossless 1.34x decode speedup on CUDA
- Backends: llama.cpp (CUDA, Metal, CPU) + MLX companion + mlx-swift for iOS/macOS
- License: Apache 2.0

**Independent check:** Read the HF model card, whitepaper link, and GitHub demo repo. Verified the Q2_0_g128 format spec (ternary values with FP16 group-wise scaling per 128 weights). The {−1, 0, +1} claim is self-consistent — log₂(3) ≈ 1.585 bits, plus the FP16 scale factor brings it to ~1.71 bits/weight. The DSpark drafter is a separate model trained against Bonsai as target — speculative decoding at its most targeted.

**Why it matters:** This is the most aggressive quantization I've seen for a 27B-class reasoning model. At 7.2 GB, it fits on an M4 Max with room to spare. The ternary approach (vs. conventional INT2) means every weight bit carries meaningful signal — no "zero" weights that are just padding. For Karl: this is the kind of sub-4-bit model that makes local agentic inference feasible on consumer hardware without the usual intelligence collapse.

## TIER 1 — SIGNAL

### F02: OvisOCR2 — First End-to-End Document Parser to Top OmniDocBench
**Date:** July 15, 2026 (paper); July 17 (trending)
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL` ⭐
**Evidence Tier:** T3 | **Confidence:** 80%
**Sources:**
- [arXiv:2607.13639](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13639)
- [HF: ATH-MaaS/OvisOCR2](https://huggingface.co/ATH-MaaS/OvisOCR2)

**OvisOCR2 is a 0.8B end-to-end document parsing model** that generates Markdown from document page images — text, formulas, tables, and visual regions in natural reading order. Key details:
- **96.58 on OmniDocBench v1.6** — first end-to-end model to top this leaderboard (previously dominated by pipeline methods)
- **75.06 Avg3 on PureDocBench** — also highest score
- Built by post-training Qwen3.5-0.8B with SFT + RL + OPD multi-stage training recipe
- Data engine: filtered real-document annotations + synthetic pages derived from HTML source
- vLLM-compatible (vllm==0.22.1), single-GPU inference

**Independent check:** Read the arXiv paper and HF model card. The SFT → RL on 4B branch → on-policy distillation → model fusion pipeline is a sophisticated multi-stage recipe for a sub-1B model. OmniDocBench v1.6 was indeed previously dominated by pipeline methods — OvisOCR2 being the first end-to-end to top it is significant.

**Why it matters:** For Karl's document-processing workflows, a 0.8B single-model parser beats multi-stage pipelines on benchmark scores. At <1B params, it runs easily on GPU or even high-end Apple Silicon. The end-to-end approach eliminates error propagation between OCR → layout analysis → table extraction stages.

### F03: MOSS-Transcribe-Diarize 0.9B — Long-Form Multi-Speaker Transcription
**Date:** July 9, 2026 (released); July 14 (won INTERSPEECH MLC-SLM Challenge)
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL` ⭐
**Evidence Tier:** T2 | **Confidence:** 75%
**Sources:**
- [HF: OpenMOSS-Team/MOSS-Transcribe-Diarize](https://huggingface.co/OpenMOSS-Team/MOSS-Transcribe-Diarize)
- [arXiv:2601.01554](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01554)

**A 0.9B end-to-end audio understanding model** for long-form multi-speaker transcription, diarization, timestamps, and acoustic event awareness. Key details:
- **Single-pass inference** on audio up to 90 minutes long
- Supports **50+ languages**, speaker-aware transcripts with timestamps
- Won **first place at the 2nd MLC-SLM Challenge** (INTERSPEECH 2026) across 14 languages
- Jointly performs speech transcription AND speaker diarization — no stitching separate ASR + diarization systems
- vLLM and SGLang serving support

**Independent check:** Read HF model card. The INTERSPEECH win is corroborated by the model's news section. Single-pass joint transcription+diarization at 0.9B is notable — most approaches use a pipeline of separate models.

**Why it matters:** For meeting/podcast/lecture transcription workflows, a single-pass model eliminates error propagation between ASR and diarization stages. At 0.9B, it's feasible for local inference on GPU setups. The multi-language coverage (50+) is broader than most English-centric ASR models.

### F04: Wan-Dancer 14B — Music-to-Dance Video Generation
**Date:** July 13, 2026 (released); July 17 (trending)
**Signal:** `#SIGNAL`
**Evidence Tier:** T2 | **Confidence:** 70%
**Sources:**
- [arXiv:2607.09581](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09581)
- [HF: Wan-AI/Wan-Dancer-14B](https://huggingface.co/Wan-AI/Wan-Dancer-14B)

**Wan-Dancer is a 14B model for minute-scale coherent music-to-dance video generation.** Key details:
- Generates **720p/30fps videos exceeding one minute** — breaking the conventional ~20-second diffusion barrier
- Hierarchical framework: global keyframe planning + local temporal refinement
- Dynamic frame rate adaptation via time-mapped RoPE embeddings for precise alignment
- Optical-flow-based loss function for motion continuity
- Robust across **five dance genres**: Chinese Classical, K-Pop, Street, Tap, Latin
- Paper: 17 pages, 13 figures

**Independent check:** Read the arXiv paper and HF model page. The hierarchical approach (global keyframes + local refinement) is a known pattern for long-form generation; what's novel is achieving minute-scale coherence at 14B params. The optical-flow loss for motion continuity is a practical innovation worth tracking.

**Why it matters:** For the ComfyUI/image-video ecosystem, this pushes the boundary of what's possible in music-driven video generation. At 14B, it's large but feasible on multi-GPU setups. The genre versatility (5 styles) suggests broad applicability for content creation workflows.

**Independent check:** Read HF model card and discussions. The WER numbers are self-reported; cross-checking against Common Voice/FLEURS benchmarks would require running the model. The 70+ language coverage and strong performance on Central Asian languages (Kazakh, Kyrgyz, Uzbek) is a genuine gap-filler — most ASR models focus on English/Chinese/European languages.

**Why it matters:** If Karl works with multilingual audio or needs to cover underrepresented language families, GigaAM fills a real gap. The 220M variant is tiny and the 600M competitive with larger models on Russian. Fine-tuning to new languages is supported via the SSL backbone.

## SYNTHESIS & CROSS-FINDING CONNECTIONS

**The Sub-1B Multi-Modal Wave:** OvisOCR2 (0.8B OCR), MOSS-Transcribe-Diarize (0.9B ASR+diarization), and GigaAM Multilingual (220M/600M ASR) all share a pattern: doing complex multi-modal tasks with models under 1B parameters. This is the opposite direction from the 975B Inkling trend — showing that for specific domains, small models beat large general-purpose ones. For Karl's local-first bias, this is excellent news: three distinct modalities (OCR, speech, transcription) all achievable on a single GPU or even Apple Silicon.

**Quantization as Intelligence Preservation:** Ternary-Bonsai-27B achieves 95% of FP16 intelligence at 1.71 bits/weight. This extends the broader trend from last week's ThinkingCap (token efficiency) — now we're seeing that quantization itself is becoming a performance-preserving operation, not just a size-saving one. The ternary approach ({−1, 0, +1}) is fundamentally different from conventional INT2 because every weight bit carries signal.

**The Open-Weight Inference Stack Is Maturing:** vLLM v0.25.0 removed PagedAttention (legacy), made Model Runner V2 default for all dense models, and added Transformers backend parity. Combined with Bonsai's llama.cpp Metal support, the inference stack for local-first workflows is becoming genuinely production-grade.
