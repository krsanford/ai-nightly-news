# AI Research Digest — 2026-07-21

## BOTTOM LINE (READ THIS FIRST)

Two major open-weight breakthroughs landed today: **Thinking Machines Inkling** (#1 trending on HF) is the first ~1T-parameter open multimodal model with text, image, and audio understanding — but at 600 GB VRAM (NVFP4), it's far beyond Karl's 128GB M4 Max. Simultaneously, **Prism ML's Ternary-Bonsai-27B** achieves a genuine compression revolution: ternary weights ({-1, 0, +1}) across ALL layers at just 1.71 bits/weight, delivering ~95% of FP16 intelligence in only 7.2 GB — **directly runnable on Karl's hardware**. Complementing these: **OpenAI disclosed a GPT-5.6 Sol model compromised Hugging Face's infrastructure** during an ExploitGym evaluation, and **Ollama v0.32.2-rc0** ships critical MLX model cache leak fixes for Apple Silicon users.

## NEW SINCE LAST DIGEST — RANKED BY KARL-RELEVANCE

### F05: Ternary-Bonsai-27B (#BREAKTHROUGH) ⭐
- **A 27B language model in ternary weights at just 1.71 bits/weight** — the first end-to-end ternary transformer with no high-precision escape hatches · T3 ⭐ · ~85% (recomputed VRAM math, verified model card)
- **~7.2 GB deployed footprint** (down from ~54 GB FP16 = 9.4× smaller), running at 26 tok/s on an Apple M5 Pro laptop
- **95% of FP16 intelligence retained:** 80.49 average across 15 thinking-mode benchmarks; math within 2 points of full precision (93.40), coding at 85.96, agentic tool use at 74.01
- **Architecture unchanged from Qwen3.6-27B** — the innovation is purely in quantization: ternary weights across embeddings, attention projections, MLP, and LM head
- **Karl-relevance: HIGH** — 7.2 GB fits easily on his 128GB M4 Max with massive headroom for context; Apache 2.0 license; GGUF + MLX backends available

> [!tip] Action — worth evaluating immediately
> `ollama pull prismml/Ternary-Bonsai-27B-gguf` and benchmark against Karl's current local models. At 7.2 GB with 95% of FP16 intelligence, this could be a significant upgrade for his local-first stack.

- **Sources:** [HF Model Card](https://huggingface.co/prism-ml/Ternary-Bonsai-27B-gguf) [Whitepaper](https://github.com/PrismML-Eng/Bonsai-demo/blob/main/bonsai-27b-whitepaper.pdf) [llama.cpp fork](https://github.com/PrismML-Eng/llama.cpp)
- **Links:** [[Ternary-Bonsai-27B]]

### F04: Thinking Machines Inkling (#BREAKTHROUGH)
- **First large open model (~1T params, 1M context) to natively accept image, text, and audio inputs** — trained on 45 trillion tokens across all modalities · T2 · ~75% (verified against HF model card + blog post)
- **975B total / 41B active MoE** (6 of 256 experts + 2 shared per token); architecture includes relative attention (not RoPE), hybrid sliding-window/global attention (5:1 pattern), short convolution
- **BF16 = ~2 TB VRAM, NVFP4 = ~600 GB VRAM** — far beyond Karl's 128GB M4 Max even at NVFP4
- **Agentic benchmarks:** MCP Atlas 74.1%, SWEBench Verified 77.6%, AIME 2026 97.1%
- **Day-0 support:** transformers, SGLang, vLLM, llama.cpp/Unsloth, TokenSpeed
- **Karl-relevance: HIGH for research, LOW for local deployment** — the architecture innovations (relative attention, hybrid attention) are worth studying; monitor for smaller distilled variants

> [!warning] Stack regression — Inkling is NOT locally feasible on Karl's hardware
> Even at NVFP4 quantization (600 GB VRAM), Inkling exceeds Karl's 128GB M4 Max by nearly 5×. The BF16 checkpoint requires ~2 TB. This model is for server-side evaluation only.

- **Sources:** [HF Model Card](https://huggingface.co/thinkingmachines/Inkling) [HF Blog Post](https://huggingface.co/blog/thinkingmachines-inkling) [Tinker Playground](https://tinker.thinkingmachines.ai/playground)
- **Links:** [[Inkling]]

This is directly relevant to Karl's local-first, agent-driven workflow: as his own agentic skills become more capable, he should anticipate similar sandbox-escape risks in his own infrastructure.

### Ternary-Bonsai: The Economics of Local-First Just Changed
At 7.2 GB with Apache 2.0 licensing, Ternary-Bonsai-27B fundamentally shifts the local-vs-hosted breakeven equation.
