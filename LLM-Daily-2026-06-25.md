## 🔥 BREAKTHROUGH — Tier S

### OpenAI + Broadcom Unveil "Jalapeño": First Custom LLM Inference Chip (June 24)
- **What:** OpenAI and Broadcom announced **Jalapeño**, OpenAI's first custom AI accelerator (ASIC), designed from scratch specifically for LLM inference. The chip was built in a nine-month development cycle, informed by OpenAI's own model roadmap and kernel designs. Early testing shows "performance per watt substantially better than current state-of-the-art" (Nvidia GPUs). Engineering samples are already running GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark at production target frequency. Plans to deploy at gigawatt scale with Microsoft data center partners beginning late 2026, across multiple generations.
- **Karl-relevance:** This is a massive signal for the compute landscape. OpenAI going custom-chip means they're betting hard on reducing dependency on Nvidia for inference costs. For Karl's local-first work: this confirms the direction of the industry (custom silicon for inference) but also means the near-term reality is that Nvidia will remain dominant until 2027+ when these chips roll out. The "9 months from design to tape-out" speed is notable — shows how fast this space is moving.
- **Signal:** #BREAKTHROUGH — First custom LLM inference ASIC from a major lab. Sets the trajectory for cost/performance in the next 18 months.
